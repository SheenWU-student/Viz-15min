Is London A 15 Minutes City?

“Urban Future” is all about resilience and sustainability.
We believe creating a 15-minutes neighbourhood in which residents can get everything they require within minutes by walking, cycling or on public transport will ultimately improve the quality of life.
Therefore, 15-minute-city concept is the “urban future” trend.

Conclusion

1. Employment Facility: The inner London has the most accessibility to employment service by public transport, while some town centres and transport centre including Heathrow International Airport, Hounslow Town centre, Croydon Town centre, and Stratford centre also have high accessibility to employment service by public transport.
2. Supermarket: The areas around Camden Town, Bloomsbury, Holloway, Kings Cross Station and Catford have the most accessibility to supermarket facilities by public transport within 15-minutes.
3. Hospital: The borough Westminster has the most accessibility to hospital service within 15-minutes. In general, most of the places in London have zero accessibility to hospital service within 15-minutes.
4. Gp: Most areas in the inner London especially in the north bank has the most accessibility to Gp service within 15-minutes, while the outer London area has less.
5. School-Primary and Secondary: The pattern between primary school and secondary school are similar in general. The east part of London like Tower Hamlets, Southwark, Hackney and Islington have higher accessibility to schools within 15 minutes than west part. However, the secondary school accessibility level within 15 minutes is lower than the primary school accessibility level.

As shown in the "Choropleth Map", the most overall reachable borough in London is Westminster, and the least one is Havering. The boroughs that are closer to the inner London, the higher accessibility to overall service they will have. The radar map displays data of multiple facilities, comparing the performance of each borough in the proximity of different service facilities.

According to the visualisation from section "Time Map”,the points of interests are presented where Eating and Drinking are the most service in London and mostly located in the inner London. The reachable areas in a certain time range are similar between cycling and driving. Therefore, increasing cycling lane and promoting cycling are the recommended action to do in London. Moreover, the reachable areas in a certain time range are similar between cycling and driving. Cycling has a carbon footprint up to 30 times lower than that of a fossil fuel car per mile, and even less than that of walking or taking public transportation (Future.Green, 2024). Therefore, increasing cycling lane and promoting cycling are the recommended action to do in London.

A few boroughs have already achieved 15-min-city. However, there are still several boroughs located in outer London that have low accessibility to basic and essential services including Hospital, schools and supermarket. Therefore, to some extent, London can be considered as a 15-minute city, and taking public transportation, cycling and walking should be promoted more to reduce the greenhouse gas emissions.

Therefore, improving accessibility to services in these low-ranking boroughs can change London to our expected
“Urban Future”.
